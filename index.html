Recent Projects

Environmental Database and Mapping

Due to the high impact that zoonotic pathogen's have on West Africa, spatio-temporal modeling of zoonotic pathogens is critical for understanding how the risk of pathogen infection varies across space. To help aid in the modeling effort, we have developed a database that contains environmental raster data that can be incorporated into disease modeling. Our data collection is unique in that we fetch data from various sources, crop that data to the boundaries of West Africa, process the data to be consistent in time and temporal scale, and allow for easy download of the raster data. Modeling Transmissible Vaccines

Transmissible vaccines are a novel tool for limiting the spread of zoonotic pathogens within wildlife reservoirs. However, the epidemiological parameters for such vaccines have yet to be estimated. This is problematic, as understanding these epidemiological parameters is critical for determining the effectiveness of a transmissible vaccine in reducing a zoonotic pathogen. Therefore, we have paired seroprevalence data with numerical simulations and bayesian fitting methods to provide the first epidemiological parameter estimates for transmissible vaccines.
